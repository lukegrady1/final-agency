Detailed Breakdown

1. Google Search Console (Day 1)

Go to search.google.com/search-console
Add property for gradydigital.com (use Domain verification if possible)
Verify via DNS TXT record (recommended) or HTML file upload
Go to Sitemaps > Add https://gradydigital.com/sitemap.xml
Request indexing for your homepage and key pages (/services, /contact, /blog)
Check the Coverage report for any errors after 48 hours
Google Search Console is the single most important tool for SEO. Without it, you're flying blind. Check it weekly.

2. Google Business Profile (Day 1)

Claim or create your profile at business.google.com
Business name: "Grady Digital" (exact match, no keyword stuffing)
Primary category: "Internet marketing service" or "Marketing agency"
Secondary categories: "Web designer," "SEO service," "Artificial intelligence company"
Description: Lead with AI agents + local service businesses. 750 chars max. Include city/state.
Service area: List every city, state, or region you serve
Hours: Set accurate hours. List 24/7 if applicable.
Phone: Must match the number on your website exactly
Website: https://gradydigital.com
Photos: Upload 10+ photos (team, workspace, project screenshots, client results)
Services: List every service with descriptions
Attributes: Fill in all applicable (veteran-owned, appointment-based, etc.)

3. Google Analytics 4 (Day 1)

Create a GA4 property at analytics.google.com
Get your Measurement ID (starts with G-)
Add the GA4 script to the site (or ask your developer to add it to layout.tsx)
Set up conversion events: form_submit (contact form), click (CTA buttons)
Link GA4 to Google Search Console (Settings > Product Links)
Create a basic dashboard: sessions, top pages, conversions, traffic sources

4. Bing Webmaster Tools (Week 1)

Go to bing.com/webmasters
Import from Google Search Console (fastest method) or verify manually
Submit sitemap
Bing powers search results for DuckDuckGo, Yahoo, Alexa, and many AI assistants. It's free traffic with almost zero effort.

Ongoing: Link Building

Domain authority is still the strongest ranking factor. Here's how to build links as an agency without a huge budget:

Realistic Tactics

  1. Agency directories — Submit to Clutch, DesignRush, GoodFirms, Agency Spotter. Many offer free listings with dofollow links.
  2. AI tool directories — Get listed on sites that catalog AI business tools and services. This space is growing fast and many accept submissions.
  3. "Best of" roundups — Reach out to bloggers who write "best AI tools for small business" or "best marketing agencies for contractors." Pitch your inclusion.
  4. Guest posting — Write for local business blogs, trade publications (HVAC, contracting, salon), or marketing publications. Include a link back to a relevant service or blog post.
  5. Local partnerships — Partner with CRM consultants, bookkeepers, or business coaches who serve the same audience. Cross-link on each other's "partners" or "resources" pages.
  6. Sponsor local events — Chamber of Commerce events, small business meetups, industry conferences. The event page links back to you.
  7. Create linkable assets — The local SEO checklist blog post is a good start. Consider creating a free tool (e.g., "Google Business Profile audit tool" or "missed call cost calculator") that other sites will link to naturally.

Ongoing: Weekly Monitoring

Every Monday (15 minutes)

Search Console: Check Performance report. Note which queries are growing. Look for pages stuck at position 8-20 (opportunity to improve and break into page 1).
Search Console: Check Pages report for indexing errors. Fix any "Excluded" or "Error" pages.
Google Analytics: Check traffic trends, top pages, and form submissions.

Every Month (30 minutes)

Publish a Google Business Profile post (project photo, tip, or promotion)
Review which blog posts are getting traffic and which aren't. Update underperforming posts with better content.
Check competitor rankings for your target keywords. Adjust content if they're outranking you.
Ask recent clients for a Google review on your own profile.
