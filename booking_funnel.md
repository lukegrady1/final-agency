# Grady Digital — "You're Booked for Your Roadmap Call" Page

### Copy + structure doc for your post-booking funnel

This is the page a local business owner lands on **the moment they book their Roadmap Call with you.** Everything on it is built to do three things:

1. **Get them genuinely excited** for the call (not just "show up" — actually *looking forward to it*).
2. **Get them to run their free Google Business Profile audit before the call**, so you both walk in with real data to react to.
3. **Pre-sell and remove doubt**, so the Roadmap Call feels like a helpful planning session, not a sales pitch.

---

## What the original page is doing (and how we're adapting it)

Noah's page is a *pre-call indoctrination funnel.* Every section has a hidden job:

| Section in Noah's page | The job it's secretly doing |
|---|---|
| "You're Booked. Here's What Happens Next." | Confirms the booking and reframes the call as a process they're now *inside of*. |
| Watch this short video | A face-to-camera welcome builds trust. People don't no-show on someone they feel they know. |
| FAQ videos | Objection handling on autopilot — fewer reasons to cancel or stall. |
| Case studies / wins | Social proof that it works for people like them. |
| Free course | Authority + reciprocity — "this is education, not a pitch." |
| "Show up ready" + "Check your email" | Raises commitment and kills no-shows. |

**Your version changes the emotional goal.** Noah sells a high-ticket mentorship to agency owners. You sell a **done-for-you $297/mo service** to a busy local owner. So we're optimizing for two feelings:

- **Excitement** — "I'm finally going to get a real plan to get found on Google and book more jobs."
- **Safety** — "This is a relaxed planning call, not a pushy pitch."

And we're adding the thing Noah doesn't have: **a concrete action step before the call** — your free Google Business Profile audit. That's a big deal, so it gets its own section below.

Two honest constraints we design around: **you have no client testimonials yet**, and your proof is your **portfolio + the audit itself.** So we lean on real work and real data, not fake reviews.

### SECTION 3 — Step 2: Run Your Free Google Business Profile Audit ⭐

**Purpose:** This is the centerpiece action step and the most important thing on the page after the video. Getting them to run the audit before the call does four things at once: it raises their commitment (people value calls they've prepared for), it gives you both a shared screen to react to, it makes the Roadmap Call concrete instead of abstract, and the audit results themselves create urgency ("oh wow, I have problems I didn't know about").

**Copy:**

> **STEP 2** — *Do this before our call*
> ## Run Your Free Google Profile Audit
>
> Before we talk, run this quick audit of your Google Business Profile. It takes about a minute and shows exactly how you're showing up when customers search for you — what's working, what's hurting you, and where you're losing jobs to competitors.
>
> **Why this matters:** I'll be able to see your results too, so instead of guessing, we'll spend our whole call going through your real numbers and building your roadmap around them. It's the difference between a generic chat and a plan made specifically for your business.
>
> **[Button] → Run My Free Audit (takes ~1 minute)**
>
> *Already done it? Perfect — you're all set for the call. Come ready with any questions.*

**Build notes:**
- **[EMBED: your Merchynt white-label GBP audit tool here.]** Either embed the tool directly on this page or link the button out to it — whichever Merchynt's white-label setup supports. Embedding on-page is better (fewer clicks, fewer drop-offs).
- Make sure the audit captures their info so **you receive their results before the call** — that's the whole point. Confirm Merchynt sends you (or logs) each submission tied to the lead. If it can pass their email/business name through, even better, so you can match it to the booking.
- Make this section visually pop — it's the #1 action you want taken. Give the button strong contrast and consider a subtle border or highlight around the whole section so it stands out from the others.
- Keep the friction language honest: "~1 minute," "free." The low time cost is what gets it done.
- If most leads won't run it on their own, you can also frame it as required-ish: *"This is what we'll build your roadmap from — please run it before we talk."* Light pressure, but it lifts completion a lot.

### SECTION 4 — Questions You Might Be Wondering

**Purpose:** Objection handling. Noah uses 11+ videos; a busy local owner won't watch those. This becomes a short, scannable FAQ that kills the doubts that cause cancellations — especially "is this a sales trap?"

**Copy (header):**

> ## Questions You Might Be Wondering
> Most business owners have the same few questions before the call. Quick answers so you walk in clear.

**FAQ items (accordions):**

> **"Is this going to be a pushy sales call?"**
> No. We'll go through your Google audit together, I'll show you what I'd do, and we'll build your roadmap. If it's a fit, we'll talk next steps. If not, no hard feelings — you keep the plan either way.

> **"What exactly is a Roadmap Call?"**
> It's a working session, not a pitch. We look at how you're showing up online right now (that's what the audit is for), figure out what's costing you jobs, and map out the steps to fix it. You leave with a clear plan.

> **"How much is it if I do want help?"**
> One flat plan — $297 a month. Custom website, your Google profile, reviews, SEO, and one inbox for all your leads. No setup fee, cancel anytime. I'll break it all down on the call.

> **"I'm busy — how much work is this for me?"**
> Almost none. I build everything and manage it month to month. You run your business; I handle getting you found online.

> **"How fast could I be up and running?"**
> Your site goes live within 10 business days. If I miss that, your next month is free.

> **"What if I already have a website?"**
> Even better — we'll cover whether to rebuild it or improve what's there. Either way it's included in the same plan.

**Build notes:**
- Clean expand/collapse accordions. Six is plenty.
- Answers are pulled straight from your real offer, so they're accurate.
- Optional later: record a few as 20–30 second clips to match Noah's video style. Start with text — faster to ship.

## Build checklist

**Priority order:**

1. **Hero + Check Your Email sections** — even bare-bones, these cut no-shows. Ship first.
2. **Record the welcome video** — biggest single lever, and it's what hands people off to the audit.
3. **Wire up the Merchynt GBP audit in Step 2** — confirm you receive each lead's results before the call. This is the new core of the page.
4. **Portfolio grid** — reuse your existing Work screenshots.
5. **FAQ accordions** — copy above is ready to paste.
6. **Before/after results + first testimonial** — add as they come in.

**Tools:** The original is built in Lovable, so you can rebuild this fast there — paste each section's copy and ask for "a dark, premium post-booking confirmation page" matching your brand. For Step 2, embed the Merchynt audit tool directly on the page if it allows it (fewer clicks = more completions).

**The two things that decide if this page works:** the **welcome video** and the **audit completion rate.** Everything else supports those two. If leads watch the video and run the audit, they'll show up warm, prepared, and already half-sold — which is exactly what a Roadmap Call is supposed to do.
